Introduction
------------

The goal of this guide is to provide aspiring paladin tanks a comprehensive introduction towards become a successful tank for the majority of end-game content, including 40-man raids. What I define by success is being able to consistently tank the majority of end-game content such as dungeons and raids at approximately the same level as warrior tanks.  
For those who don't want to read the entire guide, skip to the TL;DR Summary sub-section.**Strengths**

* Massive multi-target threat generation.
* Excellent at tanking large groups of mobs,
* Can tank dungeons more effectively than warrior tanks.
* Extremely high single-target threat generation in raid settings.
* Large set of utility spells for aiding allies (e.g. Auras, Blessings)

**Weaknesses**

* No taunt.
* Limited access to raid-tier tanking gear.
* Almost no abilities available for single-target crowd control,
* No emergency damage mitigation abilities (e.g. Last Stand, Shield Wall)
* Limited mana resource pool for spells and abilities.

**A Brief History of Paladin Tanks:**Historically, the only viable (in terms of all raids and dungeons) tanks in Vanilla were Protection Warriors, with Feral Druids able to serve a similar, but still limited role. For Protection Paladins, their tanking abilities were even more limited, particularly due to the lack of a taunt ability. With the advent of TBC, Protection Paladins gained more tools and abilities that allowed to excel in heroic dungeons and as off-tanks in many raid encounters requiring vast amounts of AoE threat generation, but not yet able to fully replace warrior tanks in MT roles. For WotLK and subsequent expansions, Protection Paladins finally had the tools necessary to be able to compete at the same level and even excel as main tanks compared to other classes.**Topics**This guide will cover the following :

* Talents, Spells, and Abilities
* Spell rotations.
* Recommended Items. equipment, and consumables.
* Strategies for general main tank and off-tank roles.
* Strategies for tanking specific dungeons and raids.

**Disclaimers**Before we get started, some disclaimers to consider before you jump into paladin tanking:

1. Vanilla Prot paladins will generally never be able to fully replace warrior tanks as raid tanks. The paladin tanking toolkit is very limited in scope, particularly for damage mitigation as they lack abilities similar to Shield Wall, Defensive Stance, and other powerful tanking abilities. There are also a few encounters in which taunt rotations are all but necessary.
2. There are extremely few items in Vanilla WoW specifically made for paladin tanking, so paladin tanks will generally have a harder time upgrading their gear compared to warrior tanks, who have raid-tier sets dedicated to tanking.

It is well established that damage from Holy spells and abilities are a paladin tank's main source of threat, however, there are additional non-damaging spells available that can also be used for generating additional threat. Examples of such spells that can generate threat are Seals, Blessings, Cleanses, etc.

A notable benefit of utilizing blessing spam is that it can generate threat at long ranges without having to directly attack enemies. In the Onyxia video below, blessing spam is used during Phase 2 of the fight in order to remain high up on the threat table despite being fireballed, thus improving the likelihood of gaining aggro at the start of Phase 3.  
  
**Why does this work?**  
  
The tooltip for **[![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_sealoffury.gif) Righteous Fury](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25780/righteous-fury)** states the following:

*Increases the threat generated by your Holy attacks by 60%. Lasts 30 min.*

In practice, however, Righteous Fury increases the threat of ALL spells in the Holy school, such that it affects the threat generated by blessings, heals, etc. As a result, this vastly improves the threat potential of Protection Paladins, as shown in the two videos linked below.

*Righteous Fury grants additional threat on all holy abilities, not just attacks.*

**Greater Blessing Threat Generation**The rule of thumb for Holy spells such as seals and blessings is that their base threat is equal to the level that the spell can be learnt at. E.g. [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_fistofjustice.gif) Blessing of Might](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=19838/blessing-of-might) is learnt at Level 52, therfore it generates 52 threat. There may be some spells that are exceptions to this rule, which still have to be tested. Additionally, any threat genereated by Holy spells can be further amplified by [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_sealoffury.gif) Righteous Fury](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25780/righteous-fury). With the rules out of the way, this is where the threat potential of these spells comes into play.   
  
A unique property of Greater Blessing spells is that the total threat generated by a single cast linearly scales with the number of players who have the buff applied or refreshed. The more players that get buffed by a single cast, the higher the threat will be.

[**Seal of Wisdom - Mana Regeneration & Threat**](javascript:) 

Without mana, Protection Paladins cannot cast abilities necessary to generate threat.  
Paladins have several mana regenaration abilities available to them that can minimize the mana consumption rate of their rotations during dungeon and raid encounters.  
  
[![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom) is the most powerful mana regeneration ability available to paladins, and is comprised of its Seal and Judgement effects.  
  
The Seal portion has the following properties:

* Static PPM. Fixed proc rate of 15 Procs per Minute (PPM) regardless of weapon speed.
* Restores 90 mana when proc is activated.

The Judgement portion has the following properties:

* Dynamic PPM. Flat proc chance of 50% per hit regardless of weapon speed.
* Restores 59 mana when proc is activated.

While the Seal portion is expected to restore a fixed amount of mana per minute, the Judgement portion will vary based on the weapon speed. For the following calculations and charts it is assumed that all your swings will land on a mob and that the mob has no avoidance.  
  
**Formula for Judgement of Wisdom PPM:**  

```
JoW PPM = (1 / weapon_speed) * 0.50 * 60
```

Using the static Seal PPM and the dynamic Judgement PPM, the expected mana regeneration of both effects can be calculated. In the following chart, the approximate MP5 for the separate and combined portions of [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom) for varying weapon speeds are shown:![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/19188.png)  
  
As demonstrated in the chart, the mana regeneration contributions of the Judgment portion of [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom) increases when using faster weapons (lower weapon speed). Although the mana consumption rate of your rotation will very likely exceed the mana regeneration provided by [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom), at the very least you have a stable source of mana regeneration that will significantly reduce the delay between being out of mana to being able to cast another spell, inreasing your combat effectiveness.  
  
Another added benefit of using [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom) is that mana restored by the Seal and Judgment effects generate threat. Mana restored by these effects have a threat multiplier of 0.50 which be multiplied further by Righteous Fury's multiplier of 1.90. What this means is that while improved [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_sealoffury.gif) Righteous Fury](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25780/righteous-fury) is active, any procs from [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom) will generate threat equivalent to 95% of mana restored. You can multiply the MP5 values shown on the chart by 0.95 to get an idea of how much threat will be generated.

[**Holy Shield - Damage Mitigation and Threat Generation**](javascript:)

**[![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/classic_spell_holy_blessingofprotection.gif) Holy Shield](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20928/holy-shield) - Damage Mitigation and Threat Generation**  
  
Formula for calculating theoretical maximum damage mitigation from Holy Shield:  

```
Damage Mitigated = (duration * 0.4 )* [ shield_block_value + (Strength / 20) ] * 1.30  
-Variables:   
duration: time length of encounter in seconds.   
shield_block_value: block value contributed by your shield.   
Strength: total strength stat from all sources.  
  
-Assumptions:   
Holy Shield uptime is 100% and all 4 charges consumed per charge cycle.   
Dodge, Miss, Parry, and Block (from equipment) not accounted for.   
Armor damage mitigation not accounted for.   
Shield Specialization talent is maxed out (3/3) for +30% block value.   
  
-Derivation: 1 Holy Shield cycle <=> 10 seconds <=> 4 charges -> 1 block / 2.5 seconds == 0.4 blocks / second
```

Formula for calculating theoretical maximum threat generation from Holy Shield:  

```
Threat per Second = 0.4 * [base_damage + (0.05 * spell_damage)] * 2.10  
-Variables:   
spell_damage: spell damage contributions from equipment and/or buffs. +0.05 coefficient.  
base_damage: base damage dealt by Holy Shield (65, 94, and 129 for Ranks 1, 2, 3, respectively).  
-Assumptions:   
Holy Shield uptime is 100% and all 4 charges consumed per charge cycle.   
Rank 1 Holy Shield being used ( Dodge, Miss, Parry, and Block (from equipment) not accounted for.   
Improved Righteous Fury talent has been maxed out (3/3).   
Righteous Fury is active (+90% threat from Holy spells).   
Righteous Fury and Holy Shield threat bonuses stack ADDITIVELY (90% + 20% = 110%) .
```

Empirical damage mitigation and threat generation contributed by Holy Shield will likely be lower than the theoretical maximums due to the following factors:

* Number of mobs attacking you
* Damage dealt by mobs.

A decent method for estimating more realistic threat generation and damage mitigation is to multiply the theoretical values by the following coefficient:

```
coefficient = 0.33 * number of mobs  
Assuming that when fighting 3 or more mobs, all Holy Shield charges will be consumed per cycle.
```

[**Seal of Righteousness - Mechanics & Damage/Threat Generation**](javascript:)

[![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/ability_thunderbolt.gif) Seal of Righteousness](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20293/seal-of-righteousness) - Mechanics & Damage/Threat Generation**Yellow Hit and Procs:**As indicated by the description of Seal of Righteousness, yellow damage is applied alongside each of your white damage melee auto-attacks while this ability is active. This additional yellow damage has a hidden mechanic such that it acts as an additional hit with regards to being able to activate procs for weapons with permanent 'Chance on Hit' enchantments. The proc rate for 'Chance on Hit' enchantments is dependent upon the weapon speed and a fixed coefficient (varies by enchantment), generally set up such that regardless of weapon speed, the observed procs per minute will be the same. The implication of this additional yellow hit from SoR is that the expected procs per minute is almost doubled.Every time you swing your weapon while SoR is active, there are four possible outcomes with regards to procs:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **White Hit** | **Yellow Hit** |
| **Yes** | **No** |
| **No** | **Yes** |
| **Yes** | **Yes** |
| **No** | **No** |

The probability for a single swing to proc once or twice while SoR is active is the following:  

```
Proc Chance = 1 - ( (1 - P) ^ 2)  
Variables: P = Base Proc Chance (ex: 5% = 0.05)
```

**Damage and Threat Generation**Note that for Rank 3 Seal of Righteoussness, the Spellpower coefficient is 0.18, but for subsequent ranks (4 and up), the Spellpower coefficient decreases to 0.10.The formula for calculating the theoretical maximum damage from Seal of Righteousness:  

```
Damage = ( 22.075 * weapon_type_coeff * attack_speed * sor_talent_bonus * one_handed_talent_bonus) + (0.10 * spellpower)  
  
-Variables:  
weapon_type_coeff: 0.85 for 1-Handed weapons; 1.20 for 2-Handed weapons.  
attack_speed: attack speed of your weapon.   
sor_talent_bonus: Improved Seal of Righteousness bonus. Set to 1.15 if maxed out at 5 points; 1.00 if 0 points.  
one_handed_talent_bonus: Applies only if using 1-Handed weapon. Set to 1.10 if maxed out at 5 points; 1.00 if 0 points.   
spellpower: Spell Damage bonus from gear, consumables, buffs, etc..  
  
-Assumptions:   
Player is level 60.

Leveling as a Protection Paladin
--------------------------------

For leveling as a Protection Paladin, I'd recommend waiting until you're level 20 to start tanking dungeons if you have some tanking experience. At level 20, paladins can unlock [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_innerfire.gif) Consecration](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=26573/consecration) in the Holy talent tree, which is an excellent AoE threat generation ability for dungeons.

**If this is your first time playing WoW Classic or have little to no tanking experience, expect tanking dungeons to be very difficult, as it requires more situational awareness and knowledge of mob/boss mechanics compared to healing or DPS roles.**

Protection paladins are some of the best dungeon tanks in WoW Classic (if you know what you're doing) and can easily cut through levels by grinding dungeons from level 20 to 60 which tends to provide much higher experience rates compared to questing in the open world. At some points, however, it got slow at transition levels in which I was either too high or too low level for some dungeons. If you're not familiar with the dungeons, make sure to check out the dungeon guides to get yourself up to speed!  
  
Video below is an example of me tanking a 10-man Scarlet Monastery run with heavy spell cleave. Prior to the experience nerf for 10-man dungeon raids, I was able to level up there every two hours, although the experience in 5-man groups is still very good.  
  

Below is a brief outline of recommended dungeons to grind for different level ranges:

* **20 - 24: The Deadmines (DM/VC/NO U):** Regardless of what people like to call it, this will very likely be the first dungeon you tank. As a starter dungeon, it is not that difficult and it is a good place to start becoming familar with positioning and maintaining threat.
* **24 - 30: The Stockades:** A very easy dungeon for Protection Paladins thanks to Consecration and cramped hallways. Experience gain can get a little slow towards the late 20's at this point so questing is a decent alternative unless you want to do Blackfathom Depths.
* **31 - 38 Gnomergan:** A fun dungeon with plenty of good loot. Not that many challenging mechanics or bosses.
* **32 - 44: Scarlet Monastery:** Your home for the next 10 or so levels. The cramped interiors and winding passageways of the mini-dungeons make them excellent for exploiting Line of SIght to do large AoE pulls. You'll probably make friends with many mages and warlocks along the way.
* **42 - 53: Zul'Farak Zombie Graveyard Runs:** With a good spellcleave group made up of warlocks and mages, experience rates will vary from 40k to 65k experience per hour. Do be wary that the zombies will hit hard even with full plate gear, so using a mage's improved Blizzards to kite adds is a useful strategy. Towards the early 50's the experience can taper off a bit, so questing is an option.
* **50 - 57: Blackrock Depths Prison/Arena Runs:** Doing circuits through the prison and arena loops will bring good experience and loot, although there are many mobs with dangerous mechaincs. When fighting Fire Elementals, use [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_sealoffire.gif) Fire Resistance Aura](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=19899/fire-resistance-aura) to help mitigate some of the fire damage as they hit rather hard. Some of the mobs do [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/ability_warrior_sunder.gif) Sunder Armor](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=11596/sunder-armor) on you so make sure to wait for the debuff to wear off before proceeding,
* **56 - 60: Lower Blackrock Spire Orc Runs:** You're finally at the home-run stretch and can barely see the finish line! Grinding through the orcs in LBRS up until the Ogre area then resetting can fetch hefty experience rates of 60k to 90k experience per hour. Make sure your group kills the Orc Warlocks first!

Gearing
-------

### Stat priorities

**The criteria I considered for items on the list are, in no particular order:**

* **Armor**
  + Primary source of physical damage reduction when struck.
* **Defense**
  + Reduces chance of being critically hit (2x damage). Raid bosses have 5.6% Critical Strike chance.
  + Defense (soft) cap for raid bosses is 440, with 140 Defense coming from gear at level 60. -0.04% per Defense point.
  + Increases Dodge, Parry, Block, and Miss % by 0.04% per Defense point.
  + For dungeons and 20-man trash, aim for at least 360 Defense.
  + For skulled enemies/bosses, go for at least 400 Defense. Not recommended to go for Defense cap, as you will usually end up gimping your other stats.
* **Dodge/Block/Parry %**
  + Improves damage mitigation from oncoming physical attacks
  + Uncrushable (1.5x damage) by raid boss at 102.4% combined Miss/Parry/Dodge/Block %
* **Stamina**
  + Increases your health pool by 10 HP per Stamina point.
* **Spell Damage**
  + Increases damage dealt by spells, improving threat generation.
  + Becomes increasingly useful when blessing spam threat generation cannot be relied upon, such as in dungeons or 20-man raids.
* **MP5**
  + Improves mana regeneration during combat, allowing for constant threat generation
* **Agility**
  + +1% Dodge per 20 Agility points.
  + +2 Armor per +1 Agility (armor contributions from agility added after armor multipliers).
* **Intellect**
  + Increases your base mana pool.
  + +15 mana per Intellect point.
* **Strength**
  + Increases total block value by 0.05 per Strength point. Marginally improves your block value.
  + Total Block Value = Shield Block Value + (Strength / 20)

Paladin tanks will often find themselves different types of situations in which different gear setups will improve/worsen your tanking potential.   
As such, stat priorities for gear will vary across dungeons, 20 man raids, and 40 main raids, as well as other situations including AoE farming.  
Based on experience thus far, this is a tentative stat priority for different types of tanking scenarios, especially if you are progressing as a guild.

### Notable Items

Toggle to read analysis on each item.[**[![Libram of Hope](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_relics_libramofhope.gif) Libram of Hope](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=22401/libram-of-hope)**](javascript:)

[![Libram of Hope](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_relics_libramofhope.gif) Libram of Hope](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=22401/libram-of-hope) is the most useful out of all Paladin librams for tanking, due to the mana savings it can provide.  
As the tooltip states, equipping the libram will reduce the mana cost of your Seal spells by 20.  
  
**How to Acquire:**  
[![Libram of Hope](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_relics_libramofhope.gif) Libram of Hope](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=22401/libram-of-hope) has a chance to drop from [Isalien](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/npc=16097/isalien), located in [Dire Maul](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/zone=2557/dire-maul) - East, and will only spawn upon using the [![Brazier of Invocation](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_misc_enggizmos_23.gif) Brazier of Invocation](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=22057/brazier-of-invocation). For detailed information on how to acquire the [![Brazier of Invocation](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_misc_enggizmos_23.gif) Brazier of Invocation](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=22057/brazier-of-invocation), check out the [Dungeon Set 1/2 Quest Walkthrough](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/guide/dungeon-sets-1-2-quests-wow-classic).  
  
**How Good is it:**  
The mana savings from this item are far from game-breaking, but are still better than having an empty libram slot (or [![Egan's Blaster](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_weapon_rifle_05.gif) Egan's Blaster](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=13289/egans-blaster)). Depending on how often you refresh your seals and what ranks you use, the mana savings will range from marginal to decent.  
  
To make the most of the libram's effect and minimize your Seal mana cost, make sure you are specced into [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_frost_windwalkon.gif) Benediction](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20105/benediction).  
  
**Case 1: Two Seals per Minute (3.33 MP5)**  
Let's say that you are refreshing [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom) exactly every 30 seconds, such that you cast it twice every 60 seconds.  
Two Seal casts per minute means that you will save 40 mana per 60 seconds, which is equivalent to approximately 3.33 MP5.  
  
**Case 2: Seven Seals per Minute (11.66 MP5)**  
Let's say that your talent build has [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousfury.gif) Improved Judgement](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25957/improved-judgement), which reduces your [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousfury.gif) Judgement](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20271/judgement) CD from 10 seconds to 8 seconds.  
Let's also say that you are casting Judgement on cooldown, and refreshing your Seal immediately afterwards, such that you cast a Seal 7 times per minute.  
Seven seal casts per minute means that you will save 140 mana per 60 seconds, which is equivalent to approximately 11.66 MP5. At the cost of these increased mana savings however, do consider that your mana consumption rate will be much higher due to casting Judgement and refreshing Seals much more often.

[**[![Mark of the Chosen](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_jewelry_talisman_08.gif) Mark of the Chosen](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=17774/mark-of-the-chosen)**](javascript:)

Despite being a low-level green trinket, this trinket is remarkable in terms of its stat bonuses and uptime for paladin tanks, even at level 60.The +25 stats buff from the proc directly translates into:

* +1.25% Dodge Chance from the +25 Agility
* +375 mana from the +25 Intellect
* +250 health from the +25 Stamina
* +1.25 Block Value from the +20 Strength

Although the 2% chance to proc when struck does lower expectations of the uptime, based on the number of mobs attacking you and their average attack speed, the trinket's buffcan be active for the entire duration of an encounter.A formula for calculating the approximate uptime percentage of the buff is as follows:  

```
Uptime % = 60 / (50 * avg_attack_speed / num_mobs) * 100  
  
-Variables:   
avg_attack_speed - average attack speed of mobs   
num_mobs - number of mobs attacking you  
  
-Assumptions:   
All attacks are hitting you. i.e. no attacks are being dodged, missed, parried, or blocked.  
Uptime % greater than or equal to 100% indicates buff will be up for entire duration of encounter
```

As shown by the table below, increasing the number of mobs and/or reducing their average attack speed will significantly increase the uptime of the buff. For trash packs with 3 or more mobs, the trinket's effect can be expected to be active for the entire duration of the fight.![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18422.png)

[**[![Skullflame Shield](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_shield_01.gif) Skullflame Shield](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=1168/skullflame-shield)**](javascript:)

A very unique shield that shows its true potential when tanking large quantities of mobs. Its Flamestrike and Lifesteal procs gives the shield both offensive firepower and minor sustainability when it comes to AoE farming.Formulas for theoretical Procs per Minute (PPM) for the Flamestrike and Life Drain procs:  

```
Flamestrike PPM = (1/avg_attack_speed) * num_mobs * 0.01 * 60Life Drain PPM = (1/avg_attack_speed) * num_mobs * 0.03 * 60   
  
-Variables:   
avg_attack_speed: average attack speed of mobs  
num_mobs: number of mobs attacking you  
  
-Assumptions:   
Dodge, Miss, and Parry are not accounted for. E.g. 0% Avoidance.
```

At 2.00 mob attack speed and 0% avoidance, the Flamestrike PPM would increase by 1 and Life Drain PPM by 3 for every 3.33 mobs, as indicated by the following table.  
![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18950.png)Chart showing relationship between number of mobs and the theoretical damage per minute of Skullflame Shield's abilities.![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18951.png)

[**[![Force Reactive Disk](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_arcane_portaldarnassus.gif) Force Reactive Disk](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=18168/force-reactive-disk)**](javascript:)

To no one's shock, this shield synergizes beautifully with paladin tanks thanks to their Holy Shield ability. The DPS of the shield scales exponentially based on the number of mobs being tanked simultaneously.Some characteristics of the shield:

* Deals 25-35 Nature damage to each target within 10 yards
* The proc can crit based on your spell critical hit chance
* 50% chance on block for shield to lose 1 point of durability

Formula for calculating the maximum possible DPS from FRD procs.  

```
DPS = (block_chance / 100) * (1 / avg_attack_speed ) * 30 * (num_mobs ^ 2)  
  
-Variables:   
avg_attack_speed: average attack speed of mobs   
block_chance: total block chance = base block chance % + 30% block chance from Holy Shield   
num_mobs: number of mobs attacking you  
  
-Assumptions:   
Holy Shield uptime is 100% and has unlimated charges per cast cycle.   
Dodge, Miss, and Parry are not accounted for.   
Damage dealt by proc is 30
```

A unique characteristic of FRD DPS is that it scales exponentially based on the number of mobs being fought simultaneously.Below are two sets of tables and charts illustrating the FRD DPS formula in action.![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18420.png)![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18419.png)Based on the data shown, FRD excels when tanking groups of mobs rather than individual mobs (bosses).Note that max DPS will rarely be reached. For a more accurate expected DPS, multiply the max DPS by your combined Miss, Dodge, and Parry chance. To get a guesstimate DPS, multiply the theoretical max DPS by anywhere from 0.65 up to 0.80.

[**[![Thick Obsidian Breastplate](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_chest_chain_17.gif) Thick Obsidian Breastplate](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=22196/thick-obsidian-breastplate)**](javascript:)

A very unique chest piece with the niche role of being able to provide spell damage mitigation while tanking without needing to put on spell resist gear.The most opportune moments to equip this item is in dungeons where the majority of mobs are spell casters. i.e. Stratholme Living Side Cathedral, Scholomance, or Blackrock Depths Throne Room. It wouldn't be as effective against single-target boss caster enemies as the damage mitigated would be negligible.Below are formulas and spreadsheets demonstrating the effectiveness of this item, which can aid you in determining the most opportune time to equip it.Formula for calculating theoretical maximum Procs per Minute (PPM):  

```
PPM = (1 / cast_time) * num_mobs * 0.30 * 60  
  
-Variables:   
cast_time: time it takes for a mob to cast a spell.   
num_mobs: number of mobs that are spell casters  
  
.-Assumptions:   
Binary and Partial spell resists unaccounted for.   
Proc has no internal cooldown.   
Proc is consumed immediately to allow for subsequent procs shortly after.
```

![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18432.png)Formula for calculating theoretical Damage Mitigation per MInute (DMM):  

```
DMM = ppm * 400

Dungeon Tanking
---------------

Paladin tanks shine in dungeons thanks to their AoE threat generation, but they are not invincinble.

### Rotations

The spell rotation for paladin tanks is very straightforward, however, depending on the engagement, you will need to maintain high threat on a single target, or threat evenly spread out amongst multiple targets. The primary considerations about maintaining threat generation is that they have to exceed the healer's threat and damage dealer's threat so that you don't lose aggro on your targets. The first ten seconds of a pull are the most crucial towards maintaining aggro for the rest of the encounter, since if you are unable to build up enough threat within this period of time, it is very likely that you will struggle to maintain aggro on enemies.**Make sure to have [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_sealoffury.gif) Righteous Fury](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25780/righteous-fury) active prior to pulling, or else your threat generation will be ineffective.****Trash mob rotation**

1. Seal of Righteousness
   * Only refresh when buff about to expire or judgement has been used.
   * Depending on threat of each enemy, melee enemies being attacked by damage dealers to maintain aggro.
2. Holy Shield
   * Use Rank 1 to minimize mana consumption.
   * If Holy Shield and Consecration cooldowns are refreshed at the same time, go with Holy Shield, as damage mitigation is generally more important.
3. Consecration
   * Use Rank 5 for your first cast to maximize initial threat on mobs. Use Rank 1 for subsequent casts to maintain consistent threat generation and minimize mana consumption.
   * If your party is casting AoE spells on the entire trash pack to kill them simultaneously, keep using high rank casts.

**Single-target rotation**

1. Seal of the Crusader
   * Skip if Judgement of the Crusader debuff has already been applied on your target.
2. Judgement
3. Seal of Righteousness
   * Only refresh when buff about to expire or judgement has been used.
   * Depending on threat of each enemy, melee enemies being attacked by damage dealers to maintain aggro.
4. Holy Shield
   * Use Rank 1 to minimize mana consumption.
   * If Holy Shield and Consecration cooldowns are refreshed at the same time, go with Holy Shield, as damage mitigation is generally more important.
5. Consecration
   * Use Rank 5 for your first cast to maximize initial threat on mobs. Use Rank 1 for subsequent casts to maintain consistent threat generation and minimize mana consumption.

**Wisdom Rotation**  
For those that want to prioritize mana regeneration while still generating good threat. Recommended rotation.

* Maintain Seal of Wisdom and Judgement of Wisdom up on your current target at all times.
  + Use a fast weapon to maximize the amount of mana restored by Judgement of Wisdom
  + Mana restored by SoW/JoW also generates threat on your current target, so you will have additional single-target threat without having to use Seal of Righteousness.
* Holy Shield
  + Use Rank 3 to maximize threat generation.
  + If Holy Shield and Consecration cooldowns are refreshed at the same time, go with Holy Shield, as damage mitigation is generally more important.
* Consecration
  + Use Rank 5 for your first cast to maximize initial threat on mobs. You can keep using Rank 5 Consecration for much longer because of SoW/JoW mana regeneration.

### Line of Sight Pulling

Many trash packs will often contain a mix of melee and ranged enemies, which can make it difficult to keep all of them within a Consecration's area of effect and in range of crowd control abilities when necessary. Damage from ranged enemies is also generally magic based, and such spells can easily melt a tank unless they're killed quickly or kept in melee range, in which case they wlil alternate between spells and melee attacks which tend to be very weak.An effective solution to this problem is to exploit the line of sight of enemies to force them to move to you in a location where they can be kept close together. The general idea of line of sight pulling is to get within line of sight of a trash pack, them move back to a spot beyond their line of sight so they can't target you, forcing them to move to your location in which the tanking situation will be much more favorable to your party. Below is a simple illustration of how to execute a line of sight pull as a paladin tank.

![](https://wow.zamimg.com/uploads/guide/images/18252.jpg)

Simple walkthrough of the illustration:

1. Start Point/End Point. This is where you'll be tanking the mobs.
2. Drop your first consecration here.
3. Max aggro radius of mob. Pull mobs at this point then run back to your starting point.
4. End Point. Drop your second consecration here. Once all mobs are in melee range, resume your rotation.

Paladin Raid Tanking for Dummies
--------------------------------

### Prelude

They said it couldn't be done. They said it wouldn't be done. They said it shouldn't be done. They doubted it would ever be done.  
  
Recent discoveries and advances in understanding paladin mechanics have provided paladins with the tools necessary to become viable raid tanks, minus a few encounters where taunts are required as part of a tank rotation. This is primarily due to blessing spam being a powerful long-range threat generation tool that can allow paladin tanks to easily compete against warrior tanks on threat generation, and even trivialize certain encounters. As spellpower is no longer essential for threat generation in raids, paladin tanks are also able to maintain relatively high defensive stats, especially compared to fury-prot warrior tanks.

**Disclaimers**

* Using consumables is essential for improving your survivability and effectiveness as a raid tank.
* Make sure to coordinate with fellow tanks on tanking assignments and strategies. Raid tanking is a team effort!
* The massive threat generation of Greater Blessing spam is a doubled-edged sword. Recklessly spamming it for threat may result in accidentally stealing aggro from other tanks' targets, leading to widespread chaos in the raid. Using threat meters to keep track of the threat of other tanks is a must.
* Encounters that require immediate taunt rotations will still be a significant barrier for paladin tanks
* Have fun!

### Raid Tanking Rotations

The three core requirements of a prot paladin's rotation for raid encounters are the following:

* Threat Generation
* Damage Mitigation
* Mana Regeneration

Raid boss encounters tend to be rather long, so there is not much room for flexibility when it comes selecting other abilities that will satisfy the three core requirements. **Primary Raid Rotation:**

* [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Seal of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20357/seal-of-wisdom)/[![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_righteousnessaura.gif) Judgement of Wisdom](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20355/judgement-of-wisdom)
  + This is your primary source of mana regeneration. Critical towards perpetuating your rotation and not going OOM.
  + Maintain Seal and Judgement of Wisdom up on your current target at all times.
  + Use a fast weapon to maximize the amount of mana restored by Judgement of Wisdom
  + Mana restored by SoW/JoW also generates threat on your current target, so you will have additional single-target threat without having to use Seal of Righteousness.
* [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/classic_spell_holy_blessingofprotection.gif) Holy Shield](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20925/holy-shield)
  + This is your primary source of damage mitigation.
  + Use Rank 1 to minimize mana consumption.
  + To maximize your damage mitigation, it is imperative to keep this ability up as often as possible.
  + If damage mitigation isn't an issue, and threat generation is your priority, you can skip using this in your rotation.
  + The most ideal situation to use Holy Shield in is while [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/ability_defend.gif) Redoubt](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20137/redoubt) is active after being crit, as you'll be able to simultanesouly maximize your Holy Shield blocks and temporarily flatten your damage intake through reducing your Crushing Blow chance.
* [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_magic_greaterblessingofkings.gif) Greater Blessing of Kings](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25898/greater-blessing-of-kings)
  + **This is your primary source of threat generation.**
  + Over the course of a raid, you will likely consume hundreds to thousands of [![Symbol of Kings](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_misc_symbolofkings_01.gif) Symbol of Kings](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=21177/symbol-of-kings) so make sure to bring plenty.
  + Spam cast only when necessary to maintain aggro on your target.
  + At the start of fights, spam cast at least 10 times to have a sizable threat base, then cast intermitently to for steady threat generation while reducing mana consumption.
  + To maximize threat generated per cast, ensure that you're casting it on class with the highest number of players.

**Additional Considerations**

* If you are in a high-performing guild (<120 second boss kill times), it will usually be the case that tanks must prioritize threat generation over mana regeneration and damage mitigation. In such scenarios, using each GCD to its full threat potential is a must, in which case, casting GBoK will be the preferred option. If GBoK threat generation is barely keeping up with threat from your raid DPS, consider how you can maximize your passive (autoattack) non-GBoK threat generation.
* For white damage, use weapons with a higher DPS value.
* If using [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/ability_thunderbolt.gif) Seal of Righteousness](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20293/seal-of-righteousness), use a relatively fast weapon (<2 Second Swing) and maximize your Spell Damage.
* If using [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/ability_warrior_innerrage.gif) Seal of Command](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20920/seal-of-command), use a slow weapon (>2 Second Swing) to maximize damage from SoC procs.
* Avoid using [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_innerfire.gif) Consecration](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20924/consecration), as it applies a hidden debuff to enemies and uses up one of the 16 debuff slots.

### Onyxia

*Onyxia is a raid boss which paladin tanks can excel at compared to other tanks, as they are capable of consistently maintaining aggro on Onyxia for both Phases 1 and 3, as well as being able to easily round up whelps during Phase 2.*

**Tips & Strategy**

* For this encounter, aim to have about 150 Fire Resistance (including [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_sealoffire.gif) Fire Resistance Aura](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=19900/fire-resistance-aura)), and at least 380 Defense *if you want to play it safe*.
* **Phase 1**
  + At the start of the fight, make sure to have Onyxia properly positioned. Try to stand with your back aginst the rear wall of the arena.
  + Once Onyxia has been positioned, start building lots of threat with blessing spam in order to have a large threat cushion for Phase 2.
  + Try to use a [![Major Mana Potion](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_potion_76.gif) Major Mana Potion](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=13444/major-mana-potion) as early as possible in order to have a cooldown reset midway through Phase 2 or 3.
* **Phase 2**
  + Onyxia's targets for her [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_flamebolt.gif) Fireball](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=18392/fireball) ARE NOT RANDOM. Onyxia chooses her targets based on what player is currently at the top of her threat table.
  + You will very likely be hit with Onyxia's [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_flamebolt.gif) Fireball](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=18392/fireball) one or more times throughout Phase 2 due to being high on Onyxia's threat table. Each [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_flamebolt.gif) Fireball](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=18392/fireball) that hits you will reduce your current threat on Onyxia by somewhere between 50% to 75%.
  + Make sure to grab any Whelps that spawn from the left/right hatcheries with your [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_innerfire.gif) Consecration](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=20924/consecration).
  + After all the whelps have been slain, you can resume blessing spam in order to maintain yourself high up on the threat table despite being fireballed repeatedly. If possible, use another [![Major Mana Potion](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/inv_potion_76.gif) Major Mana Potion](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/item=13444/major-mana-potion) at this point.
  + To minimize the chances of being hit with a [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_flamebolt.gif) Fireball](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=18392/fireball) while building threat during Phase 2, wait until Onyxia is at 45% health or less, depending on how fast the raid DPS is.
  + You should only use [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_divineintervention.gif) Divine Shield](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=1020/divine-shield) if you are caught in the middle of a [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_fire.gif) Breath](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=17086/breath). It is not worth using [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_divineintervention.gif) Divine Shield](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=1020/divine-shield) to protect yourself against Onyxia's [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_fire_flamebolt.gif) Fireball](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=18392/fireball), since even if it fully does shield you, she'll likely target you again once the bubble expires.
* **Phase 3**
  + As Onyxia is landing, keep up the blessing spam in order to reach the top of Onyxia's threat table.
  + Even if Onyxia doesn't initially aggro after she lands, keep up the blessing spam while trying to position yourself at the spot where she was tanked in during Phase 1.
  + Once you regain aggro on Onyxia and have her properly postioned, resume your ability rotation as well as blessing spam.
  + Make sure that any Dwarf priests in your raid maintain [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_holy_excorcism.gif) Fear Ward](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=6346/fear-ward) buffed on you at all times.

Hakkar will then aggro on the player with the next highest threat until [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_shadowworddominate.gif) Cause Insanity](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24327/cause-insanity) expires on the mind-controlled tank, effectively making this a soft tank swap. Throughout the entirety of the fight, you and the other tank must be the top two on threat or else Hakkar will aggro on DPS and healers if one of the tanks gets mind-controlled.
* If you get afflicted with [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_corpseexplode.gif) Corrupted Blood](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24328/corrupted-blood), reposition yourself away from any nearby melee DPS so that they do not get hit by it as well.
* [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_corpseexplode.gif) Corrupted Blood](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24328/corrupted-blood) deals Shadow damage, so have priests buff everybody in the raid with [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_antishadow.gif) Shadow Protection](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=10958/shadow-protection) to increase the chance of partially mitigating damage ticks taken from [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_corpseexplode.gif) Corrupted Blood](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24328/corrupted-blood).
* Before Hakkar casts his [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_lifedrain.gif) Blood Siphon](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24324/blood-siphon) ability, make sure you obtain the [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_nature_regenerate.gif) Poisonous Blood](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24321/poisonous-blood) debuff by stepping into the poison gas cloud that spawns after killing a [Son of Hakkar](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/npc=11357/son-of-hakkar). Each person without the [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_nature_regenerate.gif) Poisonous Blood](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24321/poisonous-blood) debuff during [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_lifedrain.gif) Blood Siphon](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=24322/blood-siphon) will heal Hakkar for approximately 8000 health.

**Tips & Strategies**

* Ossirian starts off with the [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_curseoftounges.gif) Strength of Ossirian](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25176/strength-of-ossirian) buff (commonly referred to as Supreme Mode) which allows him to easily terminate tanks in one to two hits, even with Flask of the Titans. [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_curseoftounges.gif) Strength of Ossirian](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25176/strength-of-ossirian) increases his damage by 300%.
* The pull is the trickiest part of the fight. You can use Divine Shield during the pull so that you dont take damage from Ossirian while moving him to first crystal.
* Crystals have a 15-20 yard range and take about 3-5 seconds to activate after clicking on them. Once activated, the crystals temporariliy remove the [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_shadow_curseoftounges.gif) Strength of Ossirian](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25176/strength-of-ossirian) buff from Ossirian for approximately 45 seconds. During this 45-second window, Ossirian is easily tankable, and must be kited by the tanks to the next crystal before he reenters Supreme Mode.
* Have a spotter guide you to the next crystals. Hunters or mounted players make for excellent spotters, as they can quickly search for spawned crystal nodes and ping them on the map.
* To ensure that tanks have a strong threat lead throughout the entire fight, dont allow DPS to start until you have reached the SECOND crystal.
* Have your healers move AHEAD of you, not following you, as everybody is moving towards the next crystal.
* Take advantage of Ossirians [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/ability_bullrush.gif) War Stomp](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25188/war-stomp) knockbacks so that they throw you into the next crystals.
* Ossirian will occasionally cast [![](https://wow.zamimg.com/images/wow/icons/tiny/spell_nature_cyclone.gif) Enveloping Winds](https://www.wowhead.com/classic/spell=25189/enveloping-winds) on the current tank, forcing a temporary tank swap similar to Hakkar's Mind Control. Make sure all tanks are prepared to gain aggro on Ossirian and continue moving him towards crystals.
